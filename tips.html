Tips & Advice

Your Mind is Your Strongest Asset. Looking after your mental health is not something you should do only if feeling strongly anxious. It's actually something you need to think about all the time just like the physical health. Here is some advices on how to minimise your stress and to boost your mindfulness.

Weekly Activity Schedule

If you are battling depression or just being lazy, you might find it challenging to complete the simplest of tasks to invest in your wellbeing. Behavioural activation is one intervention that can help to combat this, therefore there is a weekly schedule down below to ensure it becomes more manageable.

Days Actions
Monday Not willing to get out of bed? Having trouble waking up? Schedule some exercise activities throughout your day, that would motivate you to make an energetic start to the week. Things such as ‘an early morning run in the park’ or ‘meditation’ can be perfect to add something different in your routine. These are perfect to stay away from Monday Blues!
Tuesday Start writing your thoughts on a piece of paper whether they are negative or positive. Scientifics believe this can be a type of coping mechanism. Expressing your emotional fallout would help you overcome the stress and anxiety. Overthinking can’t be easily stopped until you split it out. Also set a goal for each of your problems which will help you solve them effectively as it is also likely to comfort you!
Wednesday Do something fun, maybe watch a silly video? Reward yourself with a chocolate muffin? This is the middle of a week, you need to boost your energy to stay productive till the end of it. Don’t be harsh on yourself and keep staying motivated by doing something to boost your mood.
Thursday Are you achieving your weekly objectives? Ask yourself and start developing more realistic thoughts! Record any changes to analyse what you further can do about them. Personal development plays a critical part in mental health and wellbeing, focusing on your inner self.
Friday Have a phone free night! It is the most very tiring day of the week. Keeping yourself away from social media pollution is a good way of clearing your mind and starting to relax. You may read a book instead or why not engage in deep breathing sessions to wash the stress and tiredness of the working days!
Saturday Engage with others, this is a great day to socialise with friends, family members or some colleagues. You can build your own support network for a healthier life both mentally and psychically. Team games such as paintball are strongly advised!
Sunday Yey free day! Do whatever you want to do! However, don’t forget to plan a healthy diet for the start of next week. Nutrition is an important part of our lives. Studies show a healthy diet positively helps us with the effects of depression and anxiety. Set yourself up for fewer mood fluctuations!



Reading Suggestions for Adults

Reading has been shown to put our brains into a state similar to meditation, and it brings the benefits of deep relaxation and inner calm. Regular readers sleep better, have lower stress levels, higher self-esteem, and lower rates of depression than non-readers.

Author Name of the Book
Diane McIntosh This is Depression: Depression can be a lonely, debilitating illness, but sufferers are not alone, and there is always a path forward. This book is the first step on that path.
Emeran Mayer The Mind-Gut Connection: It offers a revolutionary look at this developing science, teaching us how to harness the power of the mind-gut connection to take charge of our health. Also shows how to keep the brain-gut communication clear and balanced. Mostly provides all the information you need to understand and combat this serious and isolating disorder.
Michael O’Mara Take a Moment: Beautifully designed with hand-made watercolour illustrations and small enough to be carried around, Take a Moment is the self-care companion for anyone who could do with a little help putting their wellbeing first.
Julian Brass Own Your Anxiety: Rather than trying to fight it, you are encouraged to consider anxiety as a gift. The book offers practical lessons to overcome the effects of anxiety by owning it in three key areas of body, mind and soul.
Jayne Hardy The Self-Care Project: The Self-Care Project is for those who have been feeling off-kilter for a while but have not been able to put the finger on the 'why'. It is a no-nonsense, practical journey to help you do just that. It'll walk you through the case for self-care.

Music Suggestions

Research show that music releases dopamine, the feel-good chemical in your brain. According to the findings people reassess 9% higher dopamine when they listen to music that they enjoyed. It may not be obvious to us, but there is a strong link between music and mental wellbeing. Music speaks when the words fail.
